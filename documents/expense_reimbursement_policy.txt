Expense Reimbursement Policy

Purpose
This policy explains how employees can submit and be reimbursed for business-related expenses incurred while traveling or performing company duties.

Meal Expenses
Employees traveling on approved business trips may be reimbursed for meals up to a daily limit of $60 for domestic travel and $80 for international travel. Alcohol is not reimbursable under any circumstance.

Lodging
Hotel expenses must be booked through the approved corporate travel portal whenever possible. Reimbursement for lodging booked outside the portal requires prior manager approval and is capped at the average nightly rate for the destination city.

Transportation
Reimbursable transportation includes economy-class flights, standard rail fares, taxis, and rideshare services used for business purposes. Employees are encouraged to book flights at least fourteen days in advance to secure lower fares.

Submission Process
All expense reports must be submitted within 30 days of the expense being incurred, using the company's expense management system. Reports must include itemized receipts for any single expense over $25. Reports submitted after 60 days will not be reimbursed except in exceptional circumstances approved by Finance.

Non-Reimbursable Items
Personal entertainment, traffic fines, and expenses for a spouse or guest accompanying the employee are not reimbursable.
